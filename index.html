Misinformation and Malinformation

Misinformation is false or misleading information that is spread, regardless of intent. It can be unintentional, often due to misunderstanding or lack of fact-checking. To avoid misinformation:Always fact-check sources, cross-check details, and think critically before sharing information.

Malinformation is information which is based on fact, but removed from its original context in order to mislead, harm, or manipulate. Whether something should be considered malinformation can therefore contain an element of subjectivity, and it is therefore a controversial concept.

Types of Misinformation

  • Satire or Parody: Humorous or ironic content mistaken for factual information.
  • Biased or Slanted Reporting: Information presented to support a specific viewpoint.
  • Confirmation Bias: Selective information that supports pre-existing beliefs.
  • Lack of Context: Information presented without background, leading to misinterpretation.
  • Fake News: Completely fabricated stories designed to look like real news.
  • Disinformation: Deliberately false or misleading information.
  • Deepfakes: Manipulated media content to deceive.
  • Propaganda: Information used to promote a particular ideology or agenda.

Types of Malinformation

  • Manipulated Content: Altered images, videos, or audio to deceive.
  • False Context: Accurate information presented misleadingly.
  • Imposter Content: Fake accounts or impersonations spreading false info.
  • Source Manipulation: Misrepresenting credibility or intent.
  • Selective Disclosure: Only showing parts of a story to create bias.
  • Outdated Information: Old data presented as current.

Examples of Misinformation

  • Spreading rumors without verification
  • Sharing outdated news as if it's recent
  • Misinterpreting scientific data
  • Deepfake video
  • Fake news article
  • Misleading headline
  • Outed information
  • Biased reporting
  • Conspiracy theory
  • Satire taken as fact
  • Misleading statistics
  • Fake expert

Examples of Mal-Information

  • Leaking Private Information
  • Selective Truth-Telling
  • Deeply Personal Smears
  • Context Manipulation
  • State-Backed Leaks
  • Exaggerated Crisis Reports
  • Doxing (Leaking Private Information)
  • Misleading Context
  • Revenge Porn
  • Twisting Past Actions
  • Exaggerating a Crisis for Panic
  • Leaking Private Messages to Create Conflict
  • Exposing Personal Medical Records
